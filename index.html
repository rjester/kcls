Firm Overview

In 1917 W.K. Charles Sr. (1893 -1971) began the practice of law in McCormick, South Carolina where he built his reputation suing the railroads. William K, Charles, Jr. joined the firm in 1949 and William Kimbrough. Charles, III joined the practice in 1974. When Kim joined the firm in 1974 the name of the firm was Charles & Charles Attorneys and Counsellors at Law. In the late 70's the name was changed to Charles, Charles & Scurry to reflect John C. Scurry Jr becoming a partner. William K. Charles, Jr. left the firm in 1984 to become a South Carolina Family Court Judge. John Scurry left the firm in the late 80's. The firm has always been considered as a general practice firm, concentrating in Criminal Defense, Family Law, Commercial Law, Real Estate and Estates and Trusts. The firm is now known as KIM CHARLES, LEGAL SERVICES. The firm remains a general practice but concentrates on Criminal and Family Law cases.

Attorney Profile

Kim Charles (William Kimbrough Charles, III) was born in Greenwood South Carolina in 1949. He was educated in the Greenwood Public Schools, graduated from Wofford College in 1970 with a Bachelor of Arts degree in government and graduated from the University Of South Carolina School Of Law with a Juris Doctor degree in 1974. Kim was admitted to the practice of law in South Carolina in 1974 and was also admitted to practice in the United State District Court of South Carolina and the United States Fourth Circuit Court of Appeals. Kim began prosecuting Driving Under the Influence case early in his career and became City Attorney of the City of Greenwood where he served for approximately 15 years. He was retained counsel for Palmetto Production Credit Association and several development companies. He handled many Family Court cases, including having the experience of representing the adoptive parents of triplets. In 1998, Kim closed the firm and became employed by Land Acquisition Department of Moreland Altobelli Associates, (MAAI), an Atlanta based engineering firm. While at MAAI, acquired tracts of land for roadway improvements in South Carolina and Georgia; managed SCDOT projects for roadway/highway improvements; certified titles to properties in South Carolina; and assisted in relocation of persons displaced by highway projects. In 2008, Kim returned to Greenwood and was employed as an Assistant Solicitor with Eighth Circuit Solicitors Office where he was principally responsible for prosecuting DUI's and Criminal Domestic Violence cases. He returned to private practice in 2011 where he has concentrated in Criminal and Family Law.

Kim was married in 1972 to the late Elizabeth Lee Charles (1946 -1995) of Conway, S.C. They have two (2) children, Elizabeth Charles, an Historian with the United States Department of State in Washington, DC and Will Charles, who works for the Hyatt Regency Hotel in Washington, DC.

Kim formerly served as a member on the Board of Directors for Cambridge Academy; member of the Board of Directors of the Greenwood County Alcohol Abuse Commission; member of the Greenwood County Airport Commission; twice past Chairman of St. Nicholas Speech and Hearing Center; past President of the Greenwood County Bar Association; and past President of the South Carolina City Attorneys Association.
